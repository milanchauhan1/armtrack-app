# ArmTrack Go-To-Market — First Users Playbook

*Written 2026-06-10. The one job right now: get the first 1–3 teams actively using ArmTrack.*

## The founder reality

ArmTrack is pre-launch with ~0 users. The product and the funnel (landing, SEO, blog) are built and good. **More top-of-funnel is not the priority.** The priority is manual, unscalable distribution to get the first real teams — because that produces retention data + authentic testimonials, which is what makes every other channel work later.

Think Superhuman: invite users one-on-one, onboard each personally, watch them use it. Do things that don't scale.

## The 2-week plan

1. **Make a list of 20 local programs** — travel orgs + high schools within reach. Names + a coach contact (IG, email, or text) for each.
2. **Reach out to all 20** using the templates below. Goal: 1–3 say "sure, I'll try it."
3. **Onboard each team yourself, 1:1.** Create their team, hand them the join code, walk one player through a log. 5 minutes.
4. **Talk to them every week.** What's confusing? Are players logging? What would make it stickier? This is your product feedback engine.
5. **Collect the first real testimonial** → put it on the landing page (replaces the placeholder).
6. **Post in 2–3 communities** (below) — value-first, founder-honest, not a pitch.

Everything else (more blog posts, competitor pages, paid, Product Hunt) waits behind "1 team is actively using it and would be sad if it went away."

## ORB channel map (where to spend attention)

- **Owned (built):** the app, armtrack.app, the blog. Funnel everything here.
- **Rented (use now):** Reddit (r/homeplate, r/Baseball), Facebook travel-ball + "baseball parents" groups, Instagram (coaches live here), X baseball community. Use to drive to the site — don't rely on them.
- **Borrowed (next):** pitching instructors / travel-org directors with followings; a small baseball YouTuber doing an honest look. Free access, not paid sponsorship.

## Outreach copy (ready to send)

> Principles: short, their-world-first, one low-friction ask, no fake proof, no AI tells. Read each aloud before sending.

### Coach DM (Instagram / text) — the fastest channel

> Hey Coach — I built a free app called **ArmTrack** that lets your players log their arm in ~60 sec a day, so you can see who's good to throw vs. who needs a light day before practice. I played ball and made it after my own elbow stuff caught up with me. Would you be open to trying it with your team this spring? I can set you up in 5 minutes.

### Coach cold email

**Subject:** arm health

> Coach [Name] — quick one.
>
> You've probably had a kid swear his arm's fine, then end up hurt a few weeks later — with no record of how it was actually trending.
>
> I built ArmTrack to fix that. Players log pain, soreness, and throws in about 60 seconds a day, and you get one screen showing who's ready, who needs a light day, and who hasn't checked in — before practice.
>
> I played ball and made it after my own elbow caught up with me, so it's free for players and coaches. No hardware, works on any phone.
>
> Worth a look for [Team] this spring? I'll set your team up in five minutes.
>
> — Milan
> armtrack.app

### Follow-up (4–5 days later — adds value, doesn't "check in")

**Subject:** re: arm health

> Coach — instead of just nudging, here's something useful either way: a clean pitch-count + rest-day chart by age → armtrack.app/blog/youth-pitch-count-guidelines-by-age
>
> If you do want the roster-readiness screen for [Team], it's a 5-minute setup and free. Want me to send an invite link?
>
> — Milan

### Community post (r/homeplate, baseball-parent FB groups)

> ⚠️ Check each community's self-promo rules first. Lead with the story, not the app.

**Title:** I had elbow pain as a player because nobody was tracking it — so I built a free arm-health app for teams

> I played for years and pushed through elbow pain by taking ibuprofen and telling myself I was fine. Nobody was tracking my throwing volume or how I was recovering, and it eventually caught up with me.
>
> So I built ArmTrack: players log pain, soreness, stiffness, and throws in about a minute a day, and it turns that into a daily readiness score. Coaches get one screen showing who's ready vs. who needs a light day. It's free for players and coaches, no hardware.
>
> It's early and I'd genuinely love feedback from coaches and parents here — what would you actually want it to track? armtrack.app

## Once you have 1 active team

- Swap the placeholder testimonial for a real, attributed one.
- Add real traction numbers to the site (only when true).
- *Then* it's worth building the competitor-alternative pages ("ArmCare alternative," "Ace Pitcher alternative") and the next batch of blog guides — they'll convert because you'll have proof.
